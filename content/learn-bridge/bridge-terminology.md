Contract bridge has its own vocabulary, and learning it early removes one of the biggest barriers for new players. Below you will find the most important terms grouped by phase of the game.

## The Deal

**Pack / Deck** - The standard 52-card deck used in bridge. No jokers.

**Hand** - The 13 cards dealt to each player at the start of a deal.

**Deal** - The act of distributing 13 cards to each player, or the complete play of one hand from deal to final trick.

**Board** - In duplicate bridge, the tray that holds four pre-dealt hands. The board is passed from table to table so every pair plays the same cards.

**Vulnerability** - A status (vulnerable or not vulnerable) that affects bonus and penalty scores. In rubber bridge, vulnerability is earned by winning a game. In duplicate, boards are assigned vulnerability in a rotating schedule.

---

## The Auction

**Call** - Any action during the auction: Pass, Double, Redouble, or a Bid.

**Bid** - A call that names a number of odd tricks (1-7) and a denomination (♣, ♦, ♥, ♠, or NT). Example: 2♥ = a bid of two hearts.

**Pass** - A call that requires no tricks and no denomination. A player who has nothing to say passes.

**Double** - A call that increases the scoring for the most recent bid if it becomes the contract. Used both as a penalty double (you think they will fail) and as a conventional call in many systems.

**Redouble** - A call by the side that was doubled, increasing the stakes further.

**Contract** - The final bid that ended the auction. The Declaring side must take at least (6 + the number bid) tricks in the named denomination.

**Opening Bid** - The first bid (not pass) in the auction, showing minimum values agreed in the partnership's system (typically 12+ HCP).

**Overcall** - A bid made by the side that did not open the auction.

**Preempt / Preemptive Bid** - A high opening bid (e.g., 3♥) based on a long suit but few HCP, designed to make life difficult for the opponents.

**Slam** - A contract at the 6-level (Small Slam, requiring 12 tricks) or 7-level (Grand Slam, requiring all 13 tricks).

**Game** - A contract worth 100+ trick points below the line: 3NT, 4♥, 4♠, 5♣, or 5♦.

## High Card Points (HCP)

| Card | Points |
|------|--------|
| Ace | 4 |
| King | 3 |
| Queen | 2 |
| Jack | 1 |
| 10 and below | 0 |

A full deck contains 40 HCP. An opening hand typically requires 12-13+ HCP.

## The Play

**Trick** - One round of play. Each of the four players contributes one card; the highest card of the led suit wins - unless a trump is played.

**Lead** - The first card played to a trick. The player who wins a trick leads to the next.

**Opening Lead** - The first lead of the entire hand, made by the player to the left of the Declarer, before Dummy's hand is revealed.

**Follow Suit** - You must play a card of the same suit as the card led, if you have one.

**Void** - Having no cards in a particular suit.

**Ruff** - To play a trump card when you cannot follow suit. A ruff wins the trick unless a higher trump is also played.

**Trump** - The suit named in the contract (if not no-trump). Any trump card beats any card of any other suit.

**No-Trump (NT)** - A contract played without a trump suit. The highest card of the led suit always wins.

**Dummy** - The Declarer's partner. After the opening lead, Dummy's hand is placed face-up on the table and played by the Declarer.

**Declarer** - The player who first bid the final contract's denomination; they play both their own hand and Dummy's hand.

**Defender** - Either of the two opponents of the Declarer. Defenders work together to defeat the contract.

**Finesse** - A technique to win a trick with a card that is not the highest out, by leading through an opponent who holds the missing high card. Classic example: leading toward K-Q hoping the Ace is on the left.

**Squeeze** - An advanced play that forces an opponent to unguard one of two suits by running winners in the others.

**Discard** - Playing a card of a suit other than the suit led when you have no trump (or choose not to ruff). A discard cannot win the trick.

**Overtrick** - Each trick taken beyond the number required to make the contract.

**Undertrick** - Each trick short of the contract. Undertricks score as penalties for the defending side.

## Scoring Terms

**Below the Line** - Trick points scored toward game in rubber bridge (only from making contracts).

**Above the Line** - All other scores in rubber bridge: overtricks, bonuses, penalties.

**Rubber** - In rubber bridge, the match ends when one side wins two games. The side that wins the rubber earns a bonus.

**Part Score / Partial** - A contract worth fewer than 100 trick points. The partial carries forward toward the next game.

**IMP (International Match Point)** - The scoring unit used in team bridge events. IMPs convert the raw point difference on a hand into a 0-24 scale.

**Matchpoint** - The scoring unit in pairs duplicate. You score 2 matchpoints for each pair who scored worse than you on the same board, 1 for each tie.

This terminology covers the foundation of bridge conversation. You will encounter these words in every lesson, book, and club game - knowing them cold means you can focus on the strategy instead of translating the language.
