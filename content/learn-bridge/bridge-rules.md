Contract bridge is governed by the Laws of Bridge, published by the World Bridge Federation (WBF) and updated periodically. These rules ensure fair, consistent play across all formats — rubber bridge, duplicate, and online play.

## The Deal

The deck is a standard 52-card pack. Before dealing, the deck is shuffled by the player to the dealer's left and cut by the player to the dealer's right.

The dealer distributes cards **clockwise**, one at a time, face down, starting with the player to the dealer's left. Each player receives exactly **13 cards**. All 52 cards are dealt — none are left over.

In duplicate bridge, cards are pre-dealt into boards. Players do not shuffle or deal — they pick up the hands from the board and return them at the end.

## Seating and Direction

Players are identified by compass direction: **North, South, East, West**. North/South are partners; East/West are partners. In duplicate, the board indicates which position is Dealer and which side is Vulnerable.

## The Auction

The auction determines the contract. Starting with the **Dealer** and proceeding **clockwise**, each player in turn makes a call:

### Valid Calls

| Call | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Bid** | A number (1–7) plus a denomination (♣ ♦ ♥ ♠ or NT) |
| **Pass** | No commitment — auction continues |
| **Double** | Only over an opponent's bid — increases scoring stakes |
| **Redouble** | Only over a Double of your side's bid |

### Bidding Rules

- Each bid must be **higher** than the previous bid (higher number, or same number with higher denomination: ♣ < ♦ < ♥ < ♠ < NT)
- A player may Double only an **opponent's** bid
- A player may Redouble only after their side's bid has been Doubled
- The auction ends when **three consecutive players pass** after any bid

### Passed-Out Hand

If all four players pass on the first round, the hand is passed out. No score is recorded and the next dealer deals.

## Establishing the Contract

The **final bid** (before three passes) is the contract. The player on the declaring side who **first mentioned the denomination** of the final contract becomes the **Declarer**. Declarer's partner is the **Dummy**.

Example: If South bids 1♥, North bids 3♥, and that becomes the final contract — South is Declarer (South first bid Hearts). North is Dummy.

## The Opening Lead

The player to **Declarer's left** makes the opening lead — they play one card face up to the first trick. After the opening lead, Dummy places their entire 13-card hand face up on the table, sorted by suit, with trumps (if any) on Dummy's right.

Declarer plays Dummy's cards as well as their own throughout the hand.

## Card Play Rules

### Following Suit

The most fundamental rule of bridge card play: **you must follow suit if you can**.

- If a Heart is led and you hold any Hearts, you **must** play a Heart
- If you hold no cards in the suit led, you may play any card

### Winning Tricks

A trick is won by:
1. The **highest card of the suit led** (if no trump is played), or
2. The **highest trump** played (if any player trumped)

The winner of each trick leads to the next trick.

### Dummy's Role

Dummy's cards are played by Declarer. Dummy may:
- Warn Declarer if they are about to revoke ("having none, partner?")
- Alert opponents to the meaning of Declarer's calls (in duplicate)

Dummy may **not**:
- Suggest plays to Declarer
- Criticise Declarer's play
- Call attention to irregularities except as permitted by the Laws

## Revokes

A **revoke** occurs when a player fails to follow suit despite holding a card of the suit led.

### Correcting a Revoke

A revoke **may be corrected** before the next trick is led. The player replaces the revoke card with a legal card. The original card becomes a penalty card (if played by a Defender) — it may be required to be played on a subsequent trick.

### Established Revoke

If a revoke is not corrected before the next trick is led, it becomes **established**. An established revoke results in a penalty of up to **2 tricks** transferred from the revoking side to the non-offending side, depending on how many tricks the revoking side won after the revoke.

## Scoring Basics

| Denomination | Trick score |
|-------------|-------------|
| Clubs (♣) | 20 per trick |
| Diamonds (♦) | 20 per trick |
| Hearts (♥) | 30 per trick |
| Spades (♠) | 30 per trick |
| No-Trump | 40 first trick, 30 each after |

**Game** = 100+ trick points in one deal. Common game contracts: 3NT (100), 4♥/4♠ (120), 5♣/5♦ (100).

**Slam bonuses** apply when bidding and making 12 tricks (Small Slam) or all 13 tricks (Grand Slam).

**Vulnerability** doubles penalties for failing a contract and increases slam bonuses.
