Bridge scoring can seem complicated at first, but it follows a logical system designed to reward ambitious bidding and penalise failure. This guide covers rubber bridge scoring, duplicate bridge scoring, and the relationship between the two.

## Trick Scores (Points Below the Line)

The fundamental scoring unit is the trick score - points awarded for each trick bid and made beyond the 6-trick book.

| Denomination | Score per trick |
|-------------|----------------|
| Clubs (♣) | 20 points |
| Diamonds (♦) | 20 points |
| Hearts (♥) | 30 points |
| Spades (♠) | 30 points |
| No-Trump - first trick | 40 points |
| No-Trump - each subsequent | 30 points |

### Examples

- **1♣ made** = 1 trick × 20 = 20 points
- **4♥ made** = 4 tricks × 30 = 120 points
- **3NT made** = 40 + 30 + 30 = 100 points

## Game Contracts

**Game** requires 100+ trick points scored in a single deal. The four standard game contracts:

| Contract | Tricks needed | Trick score |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| 3 No-Trump | 9 | 100 |
| 4 Hearts or 4 Spades | 10 | 120 |
| 5 Clubs or 5 Diamonds | 11 | 100 |

Making game from a part-score: if you have 60 below the line from a previous deal and bid and make 2♠ (60 points), your combined 120 total achieves game.

## Slam Bonuses

Slams are among the most rewarding contracts to bid and make.

| Slam | Tricks required | Not Vulnerable | Vulnerable |
|------|----------------|---------------|-----------|
| Small Slam | 12 | +500 | +750 |
| Grand Slam | 13 | +1000 | +1500 |

These bonuses are added to the trick score, not replacing it.

**Example:** 6♠ (small slam) bid and made, not vulnerable:
- Trick score: 6 × 30 = 180
- Small slam bonus: +500
- **Total: 680 points**

## Vulnerability

Vulnerability dramatically affects scoring. A side is **vulnerable** when they have won one game in rubber bridge, or when assigned vulnerability in duplicate.

### Effect on Slam Bonuses

Vulnerable slam bonuses are 50% higher than non-vulnerable.

### Effect on Penalties

| Undertricks | Not Vulnerable | Vulnerable |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| 1st undertrick (undoubled) | -50 | -100 |
| Each additional (undoubled) | -50 | -100 |
| 1st undertrick (doubled) | -100 | -200 |
| 2nd and 3rd (doubled) | -200 | -300 |
| 4th+ (doubled) | -300 | -300 |

Going down 3 doubled, vulnerable = -200 + -300 + -300 = -800. This is why experienced players are cautious about bidding high contracts when vulnerable.

## Overtricks

Tricks made beyond the contract score as overtricks. These score at the undoubled trick rate (e.g., 30 per trick in a spade contract). Overtricks do not count toward game in rubber bridge.

Doubled overtricks: 100 per trick not vulnerable, 200 per trick vulnerable.

## Rubber Bridge Scoring

In rubber bridge, scoring is cumulative across multiple deals.

### Above and Below the Line

- **Below the line**: trick scores from bid-and-made contracts - these count toward game
- **Above the line**: bonuses, overtricks, penalties, honours - these do not count toward game

### Winning the Rubber

A rubber is won by the first side to win **two games**. Rubber bonus:
- Win 2–0 (opponents have no game): **+700**
- Win 2–1 (opponents won one game): **+500**

The side with the higher total score (above + below) wins the rubber.

### Honours

In rubber bridge (not duplicate), holding 4 of the 5 top trump honours (A K Q J 10) in one hand scores 100 above the line. Holding all 5 honours scores 150. No-Trump honours (all four aces in one hand) score 150.

## Duplicate Bridge Scoring

Duplicate bridge uses the same trick scores, bonuses, and penalties as rubber bridge - but the **scoring comparison is different**.

### Matchpoints

In matchpoint pairs, your score is compared to all other pairs who played the same board in the same direction.

For each board:
- **2 matchpoints** for every pair you outscored
- **1 matchpoint** for every pair you tied
- **0 matchpoints** for every pair who outscored you

The goal in matchpoints is not to score high in absolute terms, but to score better than other pairs. This changes strategy significantly - bidding a game worth +620 when others score +600 in part-score is a top board.

### IMPs (International Match Points)

IMPs are used in team events (Swiss pairs and Knockouts). Your score on each board is compared to your teammates playing the same board from the other direction. The difference in points is converted to IMPs using a conversion table.

| Score difference | IMPs |
|----------------|------|
| 20–40 | 1 IMP |
| 40–80 | 2 IMPs |
| 80–120 | 3 IMPs |
| 290–360 | 8 IMPs |
| 750–990 | 13 IMPs |
| 2000+ | 24 IMPs |

IMPs reduce the impact of large swings. Bidding a thin vulnerable game worth +600 vs +170 is only 10 IMPs - not worth the risk in many situations.
