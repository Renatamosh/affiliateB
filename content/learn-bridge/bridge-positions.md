In bridge, four players sit around a square table. Each seat has a name taken from the compass: **North**, **South**, **East**, and **West**. These labels are not decoration - they define partnerships, determine the Dealer rotation, and govern who plays which role on every hand.

## The Four Positions at a Glance

| Seat | Partner | Side |
|------|---------|------|
| North | South | North-South (NS) |
| South | North | North-South (NS) |
| East | West | East-West (EW) |
| West | East | East-West (EW) |

Partners always sit directly opposite each other. North faces South across the table; East faces West. The two partnerships compete against each other for the entire rubber or session.

## The Dealer

Before the auction begins, one player is designated the **Dealer**. The Dealer has the privilege - and responsibility - of making the first call in the auction.

In rubber bridge, the deal rotates clockwise after each hand: if South dealt first, West deals next, then North, then East, and back to South. In duplicate bridge, the dealer is predetermined by the board number according to a fixed schedule.

The Dealer's position matters because the first call sets the tone of the auction, and in a close hand, acting first can be a small positional advantage.

## Declarer, Dummy, and the Defenders

Once the auction ends, the players take on one of three roles for that hand:

**Declarer** - The player from the side that won the auction who *first* bid the suit (or no-trump) named in the final contract. If East-West win the auction in 4♠, and West was the first East-West player to bid spades, West is the Declarer.

**Dummy** - The Declarer's partner. After the opening lead, Dummy spreads their entire hand face-up on the table, sorted by suit, trumps on the right (from Dummy's perspective). The Declarer then plays *both* hands - their own and Dummy's.

**Defenders** - The two players on the other side. Their goal is to prevent the Declarer from making the contract.

### Dummy's Rules

| Dummy may | Dummy may NOT |
|------------|----------------|
| Watch the play | Comment on the cards or suggest plays |
| Point out if partner may be revoking | Participate in the play in any way |
| Ask if a player has no cards in a led suit | Look at a Defender's hand without permission |
| Keep score | Distract or advise the Declarer |

## The Opening Lead

The **Defender to the left of the Declarer** makes the **opening lead** - the first card played to the first trick. This is a critical moment in bridge strategy. The opening lead is made *before* Dummy's hand is revealed, so the leading Defender must rely on the auction and their own hand.

Only after the opening lead is tabled does Dummy spread their cards.

## Position Summary

| Role | Who? | Key privilege |
|------|------|---------------|
| Dealer | Predetermined by rotation or board | Makes the first call |
| Declarer | First to bid the contract's denomination | Plays both their hand and Dummy's |
| Dummy | Declarer's partner | Lays hand face-up; Declarer controls it |
| Defenders (x2) | The opposing partnership | Try to defeat the contract |

Understanding compass positions is the foundation of every bridge hand. Once you know which seat is which and how the roles are assigned, the rest of the game - the auction, the play, the scoring - falls neatly into place.
