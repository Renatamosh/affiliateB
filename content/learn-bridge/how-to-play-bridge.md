Contract bridge is one of the most rewarding card games ever devised. It combines partnership strategy, logical deduction, and communication through a structured bidding system - all with a standard 52-card deck and four players.

This guide covers everything you need to play your first hand of bridge: the setup, the deal, the auction, playing tricks, and how scoring works.

## The Setup: Players and Positions

Bridge is played by four players in two partnerships. Partners sit **opposite each other** at a square table:

| Position | Partner |
|----------|---------|
| North | South |
| East | West |

North/South compete against East/West throughout the game. You can see your own cards but not your partner's - communication happens through the bidding system.

## The Deal

One player deals all 52 cards clockwise, face down, one at a time, until each player holds 13 cards. Every card in the deck is dealt - no cards are set aside.

After picking up your hand, you sort your cards by suit. Most players arrange them alternating red/black suits: Spades, Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs (or similar).

## Hand Evaluation: High Card Points

Before bidding, you count your **High Card Points (HCP)**:

| Card | Points |
|------|--------|
| Ace | 4 HCP |
| King | 3 HCP |
| Queen | 2 HCP |
| Jack | 1 HCP |

The full deck contains 40 HCP total. An average hand has 10 HCP. A hand with 12+ HCP is considered strong enough to open the bidding.

## The Auction (Bidding)

The auction is the communication phase. Players take turns making bids - starting with the dealer and going clockwise - to decide:

1. **Which suit will be trumps** (or whether to play no-trump)
2. **How many tricks** the declaring partnership commits to winning

### How Bids Work

A bid consists of a **number** (1 through 7) and a **denomination** (Clubs, Diamonds, Hearts, Spades, or No-Trump):

- A bid of **1 Heart** means: "I commit to winning at least 7 tricks with Hearts as trumps" (6 + the number bid)
- A bid of **3 No-Trump** means: "I commit to winning at least 9 tricks with no trumps"

Each bid must be higher than the previous one. Bids rank: Clubs < Diamonds < Hearts < Spades < No-Trump within the same number.

### Pass, Double, Redouble

In addition to bidding, players can:
- **Pass** - make no commitment
- **Double** - increase the stakes on the opponents' bid
- **Redouble** - further increase the stakes after being doubled

The auction ends when three consecutive players pass after any bid (or when all four players pass immediately, resulting in a passed-out hand).

### The Contract

The final bid becomes the **contract**. The player who first bid the denomination of the final contract becomes **Declarer**. Declarer's partner becomes **Dummy**.

## Card Play: Tricks

Once the contract is set, card play begins. The opponent to Declarer's left leads the **first card** face up to the table. Then Dummy lays their entire hand face up on the table, sorted by suit.

### How Tricks Work

A trick consists of one card played by each of the four players. The rules:

1. **Follow suit** - if you can play a card of the suit led, you must
2. **Discard or trump** - if you cannot follow suit, you may play any card (including a trump if applicable)
3. **Winning the trick** - the highest card of the suit led wins, unless a trump is played (which beats all non-trump cards)

The winner of each trick leads the next one. Dummy's cards are played by Declarer.

### Declarer vs Defenders

- **Declarer** (with Dummy's help) tries to win at least the number of tricks in the contract
- **Defenders** (the two opponents) try to prevent this - they work together to take enough tricks to defeat the contract

## Scoring

If Declarer makes the contract (wins at least the promised tricks), the declaring side scores points. If not, the defenders score penalty points.

### Points Below the Line (Trick Score)

| Contract | Points per trick |
|----------|-----------------|
| Clubs / Diamonds | 20 per trick |
| Hearts / Spades | 30 per trick |
| No-Trump | 40 first, 30 each after |

A **game** requires 100+ points below the line in one deal. Common game contracts:
- 3 No-Trump (9 tricks = 100 pts)
- 4 Hearts or 4 Spades (10 tricks = 120 pts)
- 5 Clubs or 5 Diamonds (11 tricks = 100 pts)

### Overtricks and Penalties

Extra tricks beyond the contract score as overtricks (smaller bonuses). Failed contracts score penalty points for the defenders.

## Your First Hand: What to Focus On

For your very first game, focus on these basics:

1. **Count your HCP** before bidding
2. **Open 1 of your best suit** if you have 12+ HCP
3. **Follow suit** when playing cards
4. **Lead your longest suit** when defending

Everything else - conventions, signals, squeeze plays - comes with time. Bridge rewards patience and practice more than any other card game.
