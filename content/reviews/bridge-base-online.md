Bridge Base Online, universally known as BBO, is the dominant force in online bridge in 2026. With approximately 11.6 million monthly visits, it is the closest thing the bridge world has to a central hub — and the default home for online ACBL-sanctioned games.

Founded in 1990, BBO has been the platform of choice for everything from casual club games to the world's highest-profile tournaments. Founder Fred Gitelman was inducted into the ACBL Hall of Fame in 2018. The Bermuda Bowl and World Bridge Championships are broadcast live on BBO every two years.

## Getting Started on BBO

Registering on BBO is free and takes under two minutes. Visit bridgebase.com and create an account with an email address and username. Once registered, the platform opens to a lobby showing all available game types. Most intermediate and advanced players head straight for the partnership desk, the competitive section for ACBL-sanctioned events, or the Vugraph theatre to follow live world-class bridge.

## The Interface: Functional Over Beautiful

BBO's interface is functional rather than beautiful. It reflects years of incremental development rather than a ground-up redesign, and first-time users often find it slightly overwhelming. The key areas are the main lobby (live games), the 'Casual' section (unrated games), 'BBO Stars' (robot games), and the 'Competitive' section (rated pairs and ACBL-sanctioned tournaments). After a few sessions the layout becomes intuitive.

## Robot Play: GIB and the Advanced Robots

BBO offers robot games through its GIB engine and, on BBO+, the Advanced Robots. You play as South with robot partners and opponents. The robots play to a solid intermediate standard and are excellent for declarer-play practice. Free-tier robot games are unlimited; paid robot tournaments cost approximately $2 per event via BB$ virtual currency.

## Live Play: Finding a Game

BBO's greatest strength is the sheer volume of live games at all hours. The lobby typically shows hundreds of open tables at any time, ranging from beginner-level casual tables to expert tournament events. The 'Speedball' format (each hand timed) is extremely popular for quick sessions. Finding a live game within 30 seconds is realistic at almost any time of day.

## ACBL Masterpoints on BBO

BBO is the default platform for online ACBL-sanctioned games. Most events award online masterpoints — a separate masterpoint category created in 2020 that counts toward Life Master and other ACBL ranks. Some major events also award black, silver, red, or gold points. NABC online events typically award the same color points as their face-to-face equivalent.

## Pricing: Free vs BBO+ at $5.99/month

The free tier of BBO is genuinely complete for most players — full access to live tables, Vugraph kibitz, casual games, and tournament entry. BBO+ at $5.99 per month removes ads, unlocks the advanced robots, and includes the digital Bridgerama+ magazine, with a 30-day free trial. Most casual and club players never need to upgrade. BBO+ is worth it if you read Bridgerama+ or if you play robot games heavily and the ads break your concentration.
