faq:
  - question: "Where can I find an online bridge club to join?"
    answer: "Most regional ACBL districts run online club games through BBO. You can search for clubs by district on the ACBL website (acbl.org). RealBridge also hosts clubs that have moved online. Many in-person clubs now offer regular online sessions — contact your local club to ask if they have an online program."
  - question: "Is there a bridge community on social media?"
    answer: "Yes. Facebook has several large bridge groups including 'Online Bridge Players' and the official ACBL group. Reddit has r/bridge for discussion and hand analysis. BBO has its own forums accessible from within the platform. For video content, a small but active bridge YouTube community publishes instructional videos regularly."
  - question: "How do I find a regular online bridge partner?"
    answer: "The BBO 'Partner' feature lets you request a partner for a specific session. Many players also post in Facebook bridge groups or the BBO forums to find regular partners. Playing in the same weekly club game is the most natural way to build a regular partnership — the same partners tend to return each week."
  - question: "Are there online bridge tournaments open to beginners?"
    answer: "Yes. ACBL runs stratified and strata-based tournaments online where beginners (0–20 masterpoints) compete only against players at a similar level. BBO also hosts free robot-assisted tournaments for newer players. Many club directors offer beginners' game sessions specifically to provide a welcoming environment for newer players."
