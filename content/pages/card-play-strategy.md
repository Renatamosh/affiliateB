faq:
  - question: "What is the most important card play skill in bridge?"
    answer: "Counting the hand is the most impactful card play skill for most intermediate players. Tracking how many cards each opponent has in each suit — based on the bidding and cards played — transforms both declarer play and defense. It is the foundation for every advanced technique from squeezes to endplays."
  - question: "When should I draw trump in bridge?"
    answer: "Draw trump when you have enough tricks to make the contract without ruffing in your hand, when delaying would allow opponents to ruff your winners, or when you need trump to protect your entry structure. Delay drawing trump when you need ruffs in the short trump hand, or when you need the trump suit as entries to the long trump hand."
  - question: "What is a holdup play in bridge?"
    answer: "A holdup play is when the declarer deliberately refuses to win a trick they could win, typically in no-trump contracts. The goal is to exhaust the danger hand of their long suit before they gain the lead. For example, holding up the ace when an opponent leads their long suit until the third round cuts the communication between the defenders."
  - question: "How do I signal to my partner in bridge defense?"
    answer: "Standard defensive signals include: attitude signals (high card = like the suit, low card = dislike), count signals (high-low = even number of cards, low-high = odd), and suit preference signals (high card points to higher-ranking suit, low card to lower-ranking). Your partnership should agree on when each signal applies — confusion here costs many defensive tricks."
