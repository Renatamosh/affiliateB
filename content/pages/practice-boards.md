faq:
  - question: "How do I practice bridge declarer play online?"
    answer: "The best way to practice declarer play online is to use the Funbridge practice boards or BBO's 'Practice' section, where you can replay archived hands. Bridge Baron and similar software also let you set up specific hand types to drill a particular technique. The key is to analyze after each hand — not just play and move on."
  - question: "Can I practice bridge against a computer?"
    answer: "Yes. BBO lets you play against robots for free. Funbridge has AI opponents at adjustable difficulty levels, which is particularly good for solo practice. Bridge Baron is a desktop program designed specifically for practice with strong AI opponents. All three allow you to replay and analyze hands afterward."
  - question: "What bridge hands should I practice as an intermediate player?"
    answer: "Focus on finesse positions, trump management, and simple squeezes. Practice hands where you need to count the distribution from the bidding, and hands where the contract requires careful entry management. Endplay positions — where you force an opponent to lead into your tenace — are also worth drilling regularly."
  - question: "How many practice hands should I play per week to improve?"
    answer: "Quality matters more than quantity. Reviewing 10–15 hands thoroughly — looking at your errors, understanding what the correct line was, and understanding why — is more effective than playing 50 hands without analysis. Most improving players see the fastest gains by combining regular play with deliberate review sessions."
