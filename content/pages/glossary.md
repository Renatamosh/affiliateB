faq:
  - question: "What does it mean to finesse in bridge?"
    answer: "A finesse is a card play technique where you try to win a trick with a card that is not the highest card in the suit, hoping that the higher card is held by a specific opponent. For example, leading toward a K-Q holding and playing the queen when the opponent plays low — hoping the ace is behind the king."
  - question: "What is a squeeze in bridge?"
    answer: "A squeeze is an endgame technique where you force an opponent to discard from two suits they need to protect simultaneously. A successful squeeze wins an extra trick that would otherwise not be available. Squeezes require careful counting and planning from early in the hand."
  - question: "What does ACBL stand for in bridge?"
    answer: "ACBL stands for American Contract Bridge League, the governing body for competitive bridge in North America. The ACBL sanctions tournaments, issues masterpoints, and accredits clubs. You need an ACBL membership to play in sanctioned events and earn official masterpoints."
  - question: "What is the difference between duplicate and rubber bridge?"
    answer: "In rubber bridge, the same deck is dealt randomly and played to completion, with bonuses for winning two games (a rubber). In duplicate bridge, the same set of hands is played at multiple tables and scores are compared across the field — meaning luck of the deal is eliminated and only skill determines the winner."
