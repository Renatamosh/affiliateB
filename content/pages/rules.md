faq:
  - question: "What are the basic rules of contract bridge?"
    answer: "Contract bridge is played by four players in two partnerships, using a standard 52-card deck. The auction determines the contract — which suit will be trump (or no-trump) and how many tricks (above six) the declaring side contracts to win. Declarer then plays both their own hand and dummy's hand, while the defenders try to defeat the contract."
  - question: "What is a revoke in bridge and what happens?"
    answer: "A revoke occurs when a player fails to follow suit when they could have done so. If the revoke is established (the revoking side plays to the next trick), there is a penalty: the non-offending side is awarded two tricks if the revoking side won the trick in which the revoke occurred, or one trick otherwise. Online platforms like BBO usually prevent revokes automatically."
  - question: "How does scoring work in contract bridge?"
    answer: "Making your contract earns points for tricks bid and made, with bonuses for game (3NT, 4H/S, or 5m) and slam contracts. Failing to make your contract earns the opponents penalty points per undertrick. In rubber bridge, the first side to win two games wins the rubber bonus (500 or 700 points). In duplicate bridge, scores are compared across all tables playing the same hands."
  - question: "What are the rules for online bridge etiquette?"
    answer: "Online bridge etiquette requires playing at a reasonable pace, not discussing hands during play, not asking for explanations unnecessarily, and accepting the director's ruling if a dispute arises. On BBO, using the alert and explain functions correctly is part of proper etiquette — failure to alert a conventional bid can lead to penalties in tournament play."
