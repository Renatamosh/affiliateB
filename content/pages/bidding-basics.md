faq:
  - question: "What is the opening bid in bridge?"
    answer: "An opening bid is the first bid made in an auction, signaling to your partner that you have at least a minimum hand worth bidding — typically 12 or more high card points (HCP) for a one-of-a-suit or one no-trump opening. The opening bid sets the contract discussion in motion and tells partner about your hand strength and distribution."
  - question: "What is Stayman convention in bridge?"
    answer: "Stayman is a convention bid of 2♣ after partner opens 1NT. It asks partner if they have a four-card major suit (hearts or spades). Partner responds 2♦ to deny a major, 2♥ if they have four hearts, or 2♠ if they have four spades. Stayman is one of the most useful and widely-played conventions in bridge."
  - question: "How do I know when to bid game in bridge?"
    answer: "Bid game when the combined partnership points are sufficient and the hand has a good fit. In no-trump, 25–26 combined HCP is typically enough for 3NT. In a major suit, 25–26 HCP with an 8-card trump fit is enough for game. In a minor suit, you need 28–29 HCP or a strong suit with entries. Always consider distribution, not just point count."
  - question: "What does a 1NT opening bid mean in bridge?"
    answer: "A 1NT opening bid typically shows a balanced hand (no void, no singleton, at most one doubleton) with 15–17 high card points in standard American bidding. The narrow point range and shape requirement make 1NT one of the most informative bids in bridge, and it immediately puts the partnership in a strong position to find the right contract."
