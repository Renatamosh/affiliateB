faq:
  - question: "What is the most important bridge strategy for intermediate players?"
    answer: "Counting the hand is the single most impactful skill for intermediate players. Keeping track of how many cards each opponent has in each suit — starting from the bidding — transforms declarer play and defense. It takes deliberate practice but it is learnable, and it immediately reduces costly mistakes in card play."
  - question: "How is online bridge strategy different from live bridge?"
    answer: "Online bridge removes physical tells and slows down the pace compared to in-person play. Good online players rely more heavily on the bidding and card sequence for inference, since hesitation tells are less reliable on a screen. Managing your own tempo — not pausing in revealing ways — also matters more online."
  - question: "What conventions should intermediate bridge players learn first?"
    answer: "Most intermediate players benefit most from mastering Stayman and Jacoby transfers, a basic defense like DONT or Cappelletti over opponent's 1NT, negative doubles, and a simple 2/1 game-forcing structure. Adding conventions before you are comfortable with these basics usually hurts more than it helps."
  - question: "How do I improve my bridge bidding accuracy?"
    answer: "The fastest way to improve bidding is to play more hands and review them afterward using BBO's hand record feature. Focus on borderline hands where your judgment was tested, not the easy ones. Many intermediate players also benefit from choosing one convention system and sticking with it consistently rather than mixing approaches."
